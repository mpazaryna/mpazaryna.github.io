title: Music and Yoga

---

I used to play rhythm guitar for a band called The Black Hearted Angels in Annapolis. We had the opportunity to perform at various local spots and dive bars, and it felt like we were living the dream. We would rehearse regularly, work on our original songs, and play great covers. My favorites were David Bowie's "Heroes" and Bobbie Gentry's "Ode to Billy Joe."

When I started my yoga teacher training, I realized that there were many similarities between teaching and being a musician. In both cases, you spend hours practicing and studying before standing in front of a room full of people. Preparing a setlist is crucial, and careful consideration is given to finding the right mix and flow. Just as you wouldn't want to play too many rockers in a row, you need to be mindful of avoiding too many repetitions of specific poses, which could be harmful. Each yoga studio has its unique vibe and audience, just like each music venue.

Over the years, my setlist preparation has evolved. I used to jot everything down on paper in a binder, but now I'm moving towards digital methods while still retaining the handwritten process.

Being a musician and a yoga teacher are both fulfilling roles, and I am grateful to be able to call myself both.
